Frequently Asked Questions

What data does Pulse Watch access? ▶
Pulse Watch reads heart rate, HRV, blood oxygen, sleep, steps, and energy data from Apple HealthKit. All data stays on your device — nothing is uploaded to our servers. See our Privacy Policy for details.
Why isn't my health data showing up? ▶
Make sure you've granted HealthKit permissions. Go to iPhone Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Pulse Watch and enable all data types. If you just got an Apple Watch, it may take a few hours to accumulate enough data for the daily score.
How is the daily health score calculated? ▶
Your daily score (0–100) is a weighted combination of: resting heart rate trend, HRV stability, sleep duration and quality, and recent activity levels. The algorithm adapts to your personal baseline over time — so it gets more accurate the longer you use it.
Does the app work without an Apple Watch? ▶
Pulse Watch is designed for Apple Watch users. While the app will open on iPhone, core features (heart rate, HRV, workout tracking) require an Apple Watch paired with your iPhone to collect health data.
What is OpenClaw integration? ▶
OpenClaw is an optional AI coaching feature. If you run your own OpenClaw server, you can connect Pulse Watch to receive personalized health insights and training advice. This is entirely opt-in and you control the server. Most users don't need this feature.
